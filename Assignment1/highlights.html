Snappy Hour took place at the Grove Restaurant and was one of the best evening parties of GHC! They had a mac-and-cheese bar, a line-up of chefs serving fresh food, a huge cheese table, lawn games, a spin-the-wheel game, a station for filling surprise bags for charity, a huge table of cheese, and awesome prizes! I spun to win a huge stuffy of the dancing hotdog!
As President and COO of SpaceX, Gwynne Shotwell is responsible for day-to-day operations and for managing all customer and strategic relations. She joined SpaceX in 2002 as Vice President of Business Development, and built the Falcon vehicle family manifest to more than 70 launches, representing more than $10 billion in business. She gave an incredible talk about space, showed inspirational videos, shared some information about the future of SpaceX, and told us some cool facts about Elon Musk!
The general public can visit Johnson Space Center only via Space Center Houston's open-air tram tour. The tour includes Building 30 (location of the Historic Mission Operations Control Room 2 and Rocket Park with a restored Saturn V rocket. Visiting Mission Control and learning about the first missions to space was a lot of fun, and seeing the Saturn V in person was mesmerizing! I had no idea how much fuel it took to get people into space, and how huge the rockets were!
